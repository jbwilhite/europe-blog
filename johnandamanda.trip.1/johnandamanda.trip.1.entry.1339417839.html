Monday, June 11, 2012

First Step: London

Well our plane was delayed 2 hours so we didn't make it to London until 9am.  We landed at London Gatwick which is about an hour south of the city.  While Gatwick is a bit more out of the way than Heathrow, Charlotte runs direct flights daily to Gatwick, so we saved a good bit of time and headaches by avoiding transfers. 

We made it to our hostel about 1pm GMT and had to wait an hour or so to check in.  We splurged a bit staying here, but in London (the most expensive city on our trip) even the cheap hostels are not very cheap.  Our hostel is an old house and has a good bit of character. It even has a restaurant and bar downstairs.

Even though we were tired from the flight and we lost some time from the delay, we managed to get out and walk Hyde Park and Piccadilly Square on our first day.  Unfortunately Buckingham Palace was closed for repairs.  On day two, we hit the famous landmarks: Tower of London, Tower Bridge, South Bank of the Thames, Parliament, Big Ben, Westminster Abbey, Harrod's and Soho.  We also went to King's Cross Train Station to get a picture of the famous 9 3/4 platform sign.  Amanda's sister Ellen is the biggest Harry Potter fan, and she would've killed us if we didn't get a picture for her.  We had been surviving on deli sandwiches, fruit and nuts until we went to have real fish and chips last night. 

It has been raining all day today, and we have to catch the train to Paris at 5:30pm, but we went out to Wembley to see England's national stadium this morning.  Before we go to the train station, we are going to try to get up to see Emirates Stadium, home of Arsenal.
