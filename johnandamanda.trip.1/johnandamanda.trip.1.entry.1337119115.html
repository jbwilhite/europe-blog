Clemson, South Carolina, United States
Tuesday, May 15, 2012

Hi there,
Thanks for reading our blog! I am John Wilhite and my wife Amanda and I are about to embark on a six week backpacking tour of of Europe.  Our plan is to travel cheap and experience as much authentic Europe as we can.  We will be hitting the must see cities and also getting plenty of small town and local flavor, all while (hopefully) avoiding the tourist traps.  Stay tuned for more entries as we go, but right now it's all about packing.

Because were on a tight budget, we're traveling the European way: Camping.  We will probably stay in some hostels, but camping is the cheapest (and most interesting) way to see the continent.  In the US, we think of camping as rustic settings far from cities, but pretty much all towns and cities in Europe have one or more campsites.  They have many facilities including showers, laundry, bars, restaurants, bike rentals etc.   Most are easily accessible by public transportation, which is great for us because we will be train hopping the whole trip.

For our trip, we are traveling as lightly as possible.  We are carrying camping gear that most hostel backpackers wouldn't need, so we have to go light on everything else.  We are splitting the tent between our packs, but we each have our own sleeping pad and Thermarest Sleep System.  We both have 50 liter Osprey backpacks.
