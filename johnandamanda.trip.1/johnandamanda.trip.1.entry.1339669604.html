Paris, Île-de-France, France
Thursday, June 14, 2012

Paris was our first camping spot. The campground was nice, but the weather was cold and rainy. Luckily our one season sleeping bags and complimentary airline blankets were warm enough.

Paris itself was the same as when I was here 8 years ago: grand, historic, long lines and dirty. Maybe we were holding too high of standards because we had just come from London, where they are cleaning and updating for the Olympics. Mme. Parris would be glad to know that I can still speak a little French. I could ask some simple questions, but if the answer was too long or spoken to quickly, then I was lost. Luckily only a couple of people didn't speak English, and I could still get my point across.

The first night we arrived, we just caught the last bus to the campground and had to set up the tent in the dark. The first full day we walked a good chunk of the city, from the Arc de Triomphe to the Louvre, past Notre Dame to the Latin Quarter. The highlight of Amanda's trip so far was walking around the Sephora store on the Champs-Élysées. Day two we saw the Eiffel Tower and its mile long lines, and the great panoramic views from Montmartre. It had seemed like a nice day that morning, so we washed our clothes and hung them up to dry, but it poured rain as soon as we got on the shuttle back to the campground. We had to pay €4 just to dry our clothes in the machine.

The international crowd at the campground was very interesting. It seemed mostly like families and older couples, but there was a van of Australian girls near us who seemed like they were on a European party tour. Our last night we had fun drinking wine and watching the Germany vs Netherlands soccer match with a bunch of Germans and Dutch people. I think we were the only neutrals in the campground bar. When we were using one the campground computers (Internet is a precious commodity here, probably more so than public restrooms), we overheard an Italian family's discussion on how to google search for KFCs in Paris (we saw several). The youngest son's contribution was to repeatedly yell "Kentucky! Kentucky!".

Now we're off to Belgium to escape the madness of the megacities. Hopefully even as we go north, it will warm up some (maybe the time difference here is 6 months and not 6 hours?).
