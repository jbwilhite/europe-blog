Munich, Bavaria, Germany
Thursday, July 5, 2012

Munich is the capital of the German state of Bavaria and what the average American thinks of when he or she imagines Germany. There are really tons of beer gardens, and I'm sure Oktoberfest is wild. Our stay in Munich was somewhat uneventful from a sightseeing standpoint, but was still very interesting. We ended up staying in Munich for four full days, double what we had planned, because Amanda had some sort of nasty stomach virus. As a nurse, she has a very strong immune system, so when she does catch something, it is usually pretty serious.

Day 1: We arrived early from the Salzburg train and made it out to our hostel/campground, aptly named The Tent. This was the most interesting place we have stayed yet, and definitely my favorite. I have never been to a hippie commune, but this is what I would imagine it to be like (check out The Tent's Website), complete with impromptu folk concerts around the bonfire. It is basically a large compound with 4 large tents with bunks and floorspace that slept hundreds of people, green space for tents, a cafeteria, reception, bathrooms/kitchen/laundry, tons of picnic tables, ping pong tables, a volleyball area and a basketball court. To further supported the hippie description, The Tent is very into recycling. Any glass bottle drinks you buy there, you pay an extra 1€ deposit that you get back when you return the bottle. The beer and soda companies then come and pick up the bottles to reuse. It is a very direct way of recycling. Unlike the hostels and campgrounds we have been to so far, The Tent seemed well represented by many nationalities and languages. The crowd was mostly young to middle age, but there were a few older people there too. The Tent served very inexpensive food, and I think there were many people there who didn't leave the compound, and had no intention of leaving anytime soon. After we set up our tent, we ventured to the nearby Augustiner Beer Garden, the oldest in Munich. Later we watched Spain and Italy in the final of the Euros with a huge crowd at The Tent. I've don't know what we're going to do at night now that soccer is over. Even Amanda has been getting into the matches; it's easy when the crowd around us is so animated. Congratulations to Spain for winning its third consecutive major international tournament. As a former goalkeeper, I have the upmost respect for Iker Casillas, who has not allowed a goal in an international elimination match since 2004, a total of over 900 minutes. I know Kristin was excited, by her Facebook post of simply "¡España!".

Day 2: We first went out to Allianz Arena, Bayern Mûnchen's new stadium. It was here that Amanda started not feeling well, and we went back to The Tent. After she rested, she felt better and we worked some on our itinerary and budget. We have been monitoring the weather over Switzerland for a few days, and it looked bad enough that we considered rerouting and coming back up through Italy in a week or so. But any solution we came up with was too time consuming and expensive. It is nice that we are flexible enough to even consider it though. The weather was supposed to be bad in Munich too, but we only got rain in the evenings. We are hoping the same holds true for Switzerland.

Day 3: Amanda woke up feeling terrible. She threw up a couple times in the morning. We had to scrap our plans of doing anything that day so she could rest. In the afternoon, she felt well enough to go with me to the grocery store and pharmacy, but she ended up throwing up on the sidewalk as soon as we got off the tram. We got some drugs for her and some food, then went back to The Tent for her to rest more. While she was sleeping, I ended up playing basketball with a Libyan-Canadian guy who was part of the freedom fighters who ousted Gaddafi, and he told me how much better life is for the people there. He now works as a car importer, and he was bringing BMWs back to Libya. Later, I talked to a German guy about the differences between the way American and German sports leagues work. His English wasn't the best, but I talked slowly and didn't use big words, and we had a good conversation. At the tent, we ran into the Australian guy we met on the night train from Amsterdam to Copenhagen a couple weeks earlier. Amazingly, not only did he see us again, but in Prague, he ran into the Swiss couple from our same cabin. Earlier in the day, the Swiss couple had run into the Australian lady. So all six members of our cabin bumped into another member down the road. He had been to Berlin, Prague and Krakow before ending up in Munich. He said Krakow was really nice except for the heat. Of all the travelers we have talked to, Krakow has been the most recommended destination. That's interesting because Krakow was not on our radar at all during our planning.

Day 4: Amanda woke up feeling much better. She still wasn't 100%, but we did make it into the city for some sightseeing. We walked around the Olympic Park area first. It's hard to believe the complex is over 40 years old; it was a revolutionary building system at the time, and still looks modern. We ate lunch and walked through the English Gardens, famous for its nude sunbathers and surfers. We did see some nude sunbathers, but it was mostly old men with too little shame. It is odd to think of surfers in Munich, but there is a rapid on the river in the park that forms a surfable wave. We sat for a while and watched them take turns; it was one of the coolest things we have seen yet (Video coming when I can get it uploaded). We saw the main square, Marienplatz, and I had a way too big beer at the place-you-just-kinda-have-to-go-to when you're in Munich, the Hofbrauhaus. It is a giant beer hall that doesn't offer it's two primary beers in anything smaller than 1 liter steins.

I feel like we basically lived at the Tent for 4 days, but that's okay. As the cheapest and most interesting place we stayed, it helped our budgets and we certainly weren't bored. We didn't get to see Dachau, the first concentration camp, but I feel like we experienced the city. Hopefully the weather holds out in Switzerland, and Amanda can shake off the last bit of this bug on our long train ride day.
